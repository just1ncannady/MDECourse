Module 1 Overview

Module 1

Module Outline and Content:

  • Exploration (20 min)
  • Insights (15 min)
  • Discussion (20 min)
  • Synthesis (20 min)
  • Self-Check (10 min)
  • Reflection and Next Steps (5 min)

In this module, you will examine your personal biases and understand how these can influence classroom dynamics. By exploring the broader societal impacts of computing, you will learn strategies to minimize threats to inclusion and foster a positive classroom climate. You will work toward creating a Positive Classroom Planning Guide (an artifact adapted from Identity Conscious Educator by Liza Talusan) that can help you implement inclusive practices for a supportive learning environment.

The CS content focus for this module will be analyzing the impacts of computing with emphasis on the corresponding CS practices, helping you foster an inclusive computing culture and collaborating with others around computing.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

  • 1.1.A - Explain how personal biases and societal factors influence perceptions of who belongs in CS.
  • 1.2.A - All: Discuss real-world examples of computing’s societal impacts.
    • 1.2.E - K - 5 (Elem): Discuss computing technologies that have changed the world, and express how those technologies influence, and are influenced by, cultural practices (1B-IC-18).
    • 1.2.M - 6 - 8 (Mid): Discuss issues of bias and accessibility in the design of existing technologies (2-IC-21).
    • 1.2.H - 9 - 12 (High): Discuss the ways computing impacts personal, ethical, social, economic, and cultural practices.
  • 1.3.A - Identify inclusive classroom strategies to create a Positive Classroom Planning Guide that proactively addresses threats to inclusion (Learning Artifact).
